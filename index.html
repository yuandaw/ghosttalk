GhostTalk: Interactive Attack on Smartphone Voice Assistant Through Power Line Side-Channel

GhostTalk is a new interactive attack aiming at the smartphone voice assistants, like Siri and Google Assistant. When the user is charging the phone by a modified power bank, attackers can remotely control the phone, such as making phone calls and stealing private information.

Here are some natural voice samples and injected voice samples. Can you distinguish them?

Also, we notice that charging voltage contains audio signal leakage from smartphone loudspeaker. To extend the attack scenario of GhostTalk, we introduce GhostTalk-SC, an adaptive eavesdropper system targeting smartphones charged in public USB ports.

GhostTalk Attack Demo Video.

By implementing a DNN model, GhostTalk-SC can recognize spoken digits in the audio and hack passwords, CVV numbers or verfication codes.
